# Numerical Analysis – April 2026 B

This repository contains my e-portfolio evidence for the Numerical Analysis module.

## Statistical Analysis Evidence

The analysis was carried out in RStudio using data imported from an SPSS `.sav` file.

The following statistical tasks were completed:

1. Measures of central tendency for the age variable.
2. Comparison of median diastolic blood pressure between diabetic and non-diabetic participants.
3. Comparison of systolic blood pressure across occupational groups.

## Summary of Findings

The age variable was summarised using measures of central tendency. The mean age was 26.51 years, the median age was 27 years, and the mode was 26 years.

A Wilcoxon rank-sum test was conducted to examine whether median diastolic blood pressure differed between diabetic and non-diabetic participants. The result was not statistically significant, W = 3804.5, p = 0.7999. Therefore, there was no evidence to suggest that median diastolic blood pressure differed between diabetic and non-diabetic participants.

A one-way ANOVA was conducted to examine whether systolic blood pressure differed across occupational groups. The result was not statistically significant, F(3, 206) = 0.233, p = 0.873. Therefore, there was no evidence to suggest that mean systolic blood pressure differed across occupational groups.
